BIOGRAPHY

ORIGIN

Born in Shanghai in 1958, Wong Kar-wai moved to Hong Kong at the age of five. Growing up in the crowded streets of Tsim Sha Tsui, he developed a fascination with cinema as an escape from reality. The neon-lit nights and the transient nature of urban life would later become hallmarks of his visual language.

After studying graphic design at Hong Kong Polytechnic, Wong began his career as a screenwriter in the early 1980s. His scripts were known for their unconventional narratives and deep emotional undercurrents, setting him apart from his contemporaries in the Hong Kong film industry.

THE AUTEUR

Wong made his directorial debut with As Tears Go By in 1988, but it was Days of Being Wild (1990) that established his signature style: fractured narratives, unrequited love, and characters adrift in time. His collaboration with cinematographer Christopher Doyle became legendary, creating some of cinema's most breathtaking images.

Throughout the 1990s, Wong crafted a remarkable trilogy of urban loneliness with Chungking Express (1994), Fallen Angels (1995), and Happy Together (1997). His masterpiece In the Mood for Love (2000) brought international acclaim, winning the Technical Grand Prize at Cannes and cementing his status as one of world cinema's greatest poets of time and memory.
